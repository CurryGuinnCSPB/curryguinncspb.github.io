The Applied Computer Science Post-Baccalaureate program at the University of Colorado Boulder is an online computer science degree designed for students who already hold a bachelor’s degree in another field. Many students in the program are making a serious transition, either into software development, data-oriented roles, or graduate study in computer science.

I teach in the program and work on student experience initiatives connected to advising, pathways, alumni outcomes, and outreach. This page is meant as a practical companion to the official university site. It gives a faculty perspective on what the program is, what students do here, and how the pieces fit together.

## Why this page exists

The official university pages are the authoritative source for admissions, policies, and degree requirements. My goal here is a little different. I want to make the structure and character of the program easier to understand from the perspective of someone teaching in it.

In general, the program works especially well for students who are motivated, willing to build strong foundations, and interested in developing both technical skill and professional judgment. One thing to keep in mind is that the program asks students to do real computer science work. That is part of what makes it valuable.
